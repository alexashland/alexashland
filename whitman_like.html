"Liking" Whitman

Exploring Whitman's Social Network Yesterday, Today

What Would Whitman "Like"?

Retrofitting social networking protocols to accommodate nineteenth-century poets

  • Network Link

This is a pedagogical exercise which encourages students to think about the social, political, and cultural environment of the nineteenth century using contemporary social networking tools. It aims to put Whitman in conversation with "friends" by imagining how he would have communicated with them in today's digital world.

Goals are to:

  • engage Whitman's correspondence as a means of exploring the ways in which epistolary media influenced his poetry and prose
  • think about the types of friendships Whitman cultivates over time
  • consider the historical and cultural significance of communication media

Ultimately, students (in groups) will each construct their own social network based on their assigned historical-biographical period as identified by the Walt Whitman Archive (Civil War, Reconstruction, Post-Reconstruction, Old Age). After, we will use social networking programs like Gephi and Google Fusion Tables to further examine the relationships Whitman cultivates.
