University of Iowa

Reading Group

Blog

A group of University of Iowa PhD students got together to read nineteenth- and twentieth-century literature, and then decided to make it a semi-regular habit. Really, though, the blog was started to help students prepare for the Comprehensive Exam that they will take in their third or fourth year of the PhD program. The idea behind the blog is that each week one member will generate a post that others can then comment on or share insights. Comps asks a lot of students. The portfolio consists of: a five–seven page introduction, 25–30 page article of publishable quality, five substantial questions based on the Historical Period list, a 10–15 page review essay that discusses seven to ten texts, and an annotated bibliography of each of the remaining items on the Special Area list (250–300 words per entry, which comes out to about 30 annotations total), and lastly two course syllabi.

Although the reading group began with this purpose in mind, we welcome all other students, teachers, independent scholars, or people simply interested in contributing to the conversation. Each blog post should be around 750–1000 words, and we ask that responders' first comments be between 250–350 words. Going over or under is obviously not a problem (quality over quantity!), but since our end goal is to prepare for this very important milestone, we do want to keep the conversations sharp and moving.

We look forward to what you have to say!
